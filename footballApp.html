DESIGNING A PRODUCT FROM THE GROUND UP

Introduction

Koeo (acronym for Keep One Eye Open) is a premium Stream & Search app that lets you watch football matches, and interact directly with players, coaches and like-minded football fans.

Affordability and freedom of a monthly fee are the greatest benefit Koeo offers for the true football fans!

Goals

Koeo aims to close the gap between fans and players, providing interaction during a match stream, and a greater sense of virtual community.

Football entities such as players and coaches are verified and are automatically granted mod access. This ensures control for the professionals and behavioural responsabilities for the fans

The overall goal of this project is to showcase that as a Designer i am fluid, and i can adapt with the work at hand. This includes interface design, user research, illustration and front-end coding (this website).

Ideation & validation

This project was born from failure. A failed challenge for a company i have applied. Despite not getting the position, i really liked the idea and as such, i took the motive and opportunity to build something that i believe in.

I had the general idea of the product down, but i had a million questions and like...three answers!

The process

I sought to answer the main questions i had:

  • The problem: is this problem worth solving? Do users think this is a major problem? Is my solution appealing?
  • The market: Some users might agree that this is a problem worth solving. But are there enough of them to make up a market for my product?
  • The product: The problem might exist, but does my product actually solve it?
  • Willingness to pay: There is market demand and a great product idea. But will people actually be willing to reach into their wallets and pay for it?

Conducting user research

I was fairly confident in the product idea, but i first needed to figure out whether the problem is a real one that needs solving. To do this, my best option was to talk to potential users directly. The focus here is on getting qualitative validation of the product idea.

I found five people (complete strangers to me, 3 Uber drivers and 2 random people) who said they would be keen to use my hypothetical product. While it doesn’t necessarily guarantee that the problem actually exists or that my proposed solution is good or valuable, it was the simplest way to begin validating the problem i’ve identified.

To broaden my research, i've made a survey and posted on Reddit Portugal. With the survey i gathered quantitative data to build my persona.

UX Design

I did a research of indicators of live-stream engagement, focusing in how psychologically attached the viewer is to live-stream platforms and specific live-stream communities. I had Twitch as reference.

User persona is a method / tool created in product design and development process to represent the characters of a group pf people. It answers the questions like “For whom we design the product?” and “What are the characters of this group of people?” which enable the product made to meet the real needs of its users. User personas also make the product design process easier by offering a vivid character of users. Based on the data gathered the persona below was developed.

Journey mapping is a tool used often at the analysis phase of the design process to understand how a user would experience the product, from discovering the product to possibly coming back to it after their first time use.

User Flow refers to the process in which the user takes advantage of the compound routes of the series of templates designed in a product to accomplish their goal. It is created to predict and show the possible routes with which the user interacts with the product.

At this stage i validated the actions with a small sample of users.

Designing wireframes & illustrations

When all the steps above were achieved, i went to the stage of wireframing. A wireframe is a simplified and schematic visual representation of a layout for app screens and transitions. It is a fast way to plan the information architecture and ensure that developers and clients get a clear understanding of the project structure.

To further reinforce the product, i built a brand. The logo is based on the shape of an eye and the iris is a football. So keep one eye open for football!

The tutorials appear to users who launch a mobile app for the first time helping them get oriented within unfamiliar features and controls.
